About Me

I treat training as behavioral intervention, not content delivery. My MA in Clinical Psychology combined with a PhD in Learning Sciences gives me a unique lens for understanding how people learn, believe, and change.

At ASML, I led enterprise-scale blended learning initiatives: designed a global functional learning program that reduced new-hire ramp-up time by 15%, increased adoption by 40%, and drove engagement up 65%.

My dissertation research examined how provisional language and prosodic cues affect veridicality judgments using mouse-tracking and mixed-effects modeling—a deep dive into the cognitive science behind persuasion and trust.
